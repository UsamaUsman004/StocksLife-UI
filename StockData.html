Stock Data

    The Standard & Poor’s 500, or famously known as the S&P 500, is an American Stock Market Index comprising of 500 large companies. All these companies have their stocks listed either in NYSE or NASDAQ. The S&P is widely used as a measure of the general level of stock prices, as it includes both growth stocks and value stocks.

    Many investors follow the S&P 500, this is also considered as one of the best representations of the US Stock market. The common stocks have been classified as the leading indicators of business cycle by the National Bureau of Economic Research.

    The New York Stock Exchange (NYSE) is an American Stock Exchange located in Lower Manhattan at 11 Wall Street in New York City, New York. It is by far the World’s largest stock exchange by Market Capitalization of its listed companies at US$21.3 trillion as of June 2017.

It is an American Stock Exchange, the Second largest in the World by Market Capitalization, also in the heart of Manhattan New York. Nasdaq was the first Stock Exchange to start trading on-line. Nasdaq is home of many popular US based companies such as Apple, Microsoft, Oracle and Dell.

Nasdaq has an index, which is comprising of Large Cap Companies called Nasdaq-100, this was first introduced back in 1985. This index tracks the 100 largest companies in terms of market capitalization.

The Dow Jones Industrial Average (DJIA), or just Dow, is also a Stock Market Index comprising of 30 large and publicly owned companies based in United States. DOW is also one of the oldest U.S. Market index.

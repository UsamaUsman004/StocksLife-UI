How Stocks Work?

It is a simple concept. Any individual who intend to start an operation such as a restaurant, and required a lump-sum amount of money, say $100,000. He invites his family members and friends to be a part of that company, and he distribute the shares of the new company as per the contribution of each member. If a total of $100,000 is raised to start the company. The valuation of each share could be assumed as $100 per share. For the distribution of $100,000.00 total 1000 shares would be required. Each member who has contributed $100.00 is entitled for 1 share of the company.

At the end of first year, if the restaurant earned 25,000 after paying the taxes and overheads including the payroll in its first year, each shareholder would be entitled to 1/1,000th of the profit. A total of $25000/- would be divided by 1000, this will bring in $25.00 earning for single share. It would be then, the decision of the board of the directors. If they want to use this profit of $25,000 to pay the dividends to the shareholders or to reinvest this profit of $25,000/- to expand the operation or to use this money to buy new equipment’s to improve the operation. It is also a possibility that the Board of Directors decide to use half of the money from the profit of $25,000 to pay out the dividends and use the other half to bring in some improvement. It has been done in the past by several large companies.

How do you select a stock?

There are various elements before someone should invest in any company.

Before you decide which stocks, you want to hold as an investment in your portfolio, it will depend upon several factors, such as:

Investing in the stock of companies with the solid and proven, long established history of sustainable increasing profitability through an entire business cycle, a company who has survived at least one recession and did not lose its shareholders trust.

Companies with management and board of Directors who are willing to return all or at least half of its profits to the shareholders in form of dividends and at the same time a company is also interested to buy back its own shares.

Investing in businesses with high returns on tangible capital.

This includes all types of physical capital such as buildings and machinery.

Investing in businesses which has significant competitive advantage which makes it quite difficult for the competitors to post any threat to that enterprise.

An important element is diversification, we all heard the story,

Never put all your eggs in one basket.

Why Invest in Stocks?

The long-term goal and a dream of each potential investor is to grow their finances through investing in potentially profitable business ventures and companies with proven outstanding performance and growth over the stipulated period of time.

What are your ultimate Goals?

A truly great investment in stocks is not a company you buy at one price and quickly sell at another, hoping for an out sized profit in a short amount of time; but, rather, one that you can buy and then sit on, as the Warren Buffet says:

“When we own portions of outstanding businesses with outstanding managements, our favorite holding period is forever.”

That is the power of investing in stocks.

Why do stock prices fluctuate?

Ask anyone about the stock market and it’s clear that almost everyone can agree on one element: the prices of stock fluctuate frequently, increasing and decreasing sometimes by shocking amounts in a single trading day.

“If you have trouble imagining a 20% loss in the stock market, you shouldn’t be in stocks”. -John Bogle
